About Me

Professor Shlomo Hareli investigates how emotions function as social signals. Based at the University of Haifa's School of Business Administration, The Herta and Paul Amir Faculty of Social Sciences, he heads the Laboratory for the Study of Social Perception of Emotions and holds a professorship in the Department of Business Administration.

His research program examines how emotions communicate social information, with work spanning diverse topics through extensive empirical investigation.

Office: Rabin's Building, Room 9136

Research Interests

Professor Hareli's research focuses on the social signal value and communication function of emotion expressions. His work spans the following key areas:

1. Mapping Social Information from Emotions

Studying the types of social information people derive from observing emotions and their impact on domains like achievement, motivation, and relationships.

2. Emotion Expression Dynamics and Social Interactions

Examining how emotion expression dynamics and unfolding social interactions impact emotion perception. Developed an influential "Frozen Dynamism" methodology for examining emotional expressions over time in controlled experiments.

3. Context Effects on Emotion Perception

Examining the influence of context on emotion perception both empirically and theoretically. Made key contributions including identifying bidirectional relationships between context and emotions, and determining that different emotions have varying susceptibility to contextual factors ("situative informativeness").

4. Personality Trait Inferences from Emotions

Advancing understanding of how inferences about personality traits like modesty, arrogance, and dominance are drawn from emotional expressions and reactions.

5. Theoretical Frameworks and Scholarly Contribution

Developing theoretical frameworks that integrate social-functional perspectives on emotion with empirical findings about how people use emotional expressions to navigate social contexts.

6. AI Applications in Emotion Research and Pedagogy

Integrating generative AI to create validated research stimuli and transform emotion perception education through AI-enhanced methodologies.

Research Innovation:

  • AI-Generated Facial Expression Stimuli: Developed a comprehensive methodology for creating photorealistic facial expression stimuli using generative AI (Flux.1-dev diffusion models with custom-trained LoRA adaptations via ComfyUI). This approach provides unprecedented experimental control while maintaining ecological validity and photorealistic quality indistinguishable from real photographs.
  • Rigorous Dual Validation Framework: Established systematic validation protocols combining computational analysis (Py-Feat) with human behavioral validation, achieving 98.38% and 97.72% emotion recognition accuracy across multiple studies. This framework ensures AI-generated stimuli meet rigorous scientific standards for emotion perception research.
  • Systematic Stimulus Manipulation: Demonstrated precise control over multiple dimensions simultaneously (emotional expressions, body weight, facial features) while preserving identity consistency—capabilities impossible with traditional photographic or 3D modeling approaches.
  • Ethical Research Advancement: AI generation eliminates ethical concerns associated with photographing or recruiting individuals with stigmatized characteristics, while enabling creation of diverse, controlled stimulus sets previously unavailable to researchers.
  • Open Science Contribution: Validated image datasets, comprehensive workflow specifications, and LoRA models openly available on OSF, facilitating reproducible research and methodological transparency in emotion perception studies.

Pedagogical Applications:

  • Interactive AI Simulations: Creating real-time classroom demonstrations where students manipulate emotional expressions and contextual variables, experiencing firsthand how subtle changes influence perception—bridging abstract theory with concrete observation.
  • AI-Enhanced Teaching Materials: Leveraging AI tools (primarily Claude) to develop professional educational content including Hebrew-language briefing documents, data visualizations, interactive exercises, and culturally adapted materials that enhance student engagement and comprehension.
  • Live Experimental Demonstrations: Conducting in vivo analyses of emotion perception experiments during class sessions, with AI assistance enabling immediate data visualization and statistical interpretation that transforms the classroom into an active research laboratory.
  • Classroom Study Replications: Guiding students through systematic replications of influential emotion perception studies using AI-streamlined data collection and analysis pipelines, teaching both classic findings and contemporary methodological practices.
  • Critical AI Literacy Development: Training students to evaluate AI-generated content, understand validation requirements, and recognize both capabilities and limitations—preparing them as thoughtful practitioners in an AI-integrated research landscape.
